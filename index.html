Rules of Auction (Zimbabwe - Timed Online Vehicle Auctions)

Important Notice

This template is based on Zimbabwe's Consumer Protection Act [Chapter 14:14] (2019), ZIMRA vehicle transfer requirements, and Statutory Instrument 59 of 2026. It is not legal advice. Auctions may require cooling-off exemptions.

1. Definitions

  • Auctioneer
  • Platform
  • Buyer
  • Seller
  • Lot
  • Reserve
  • Fall of Hammer = automatic close time
  • CVR4
  • Special Excise Duty

2. Legal Framework

  • Consumer Protection Act Chapter 14:14 applies
  • 7-day cooling-off may apply unless waived for auction sales
  • Vehicle transfer must be completed within 14 days

3. Registration

  • Must be 18+ with valid ID/passport
  • Proof of residence required
  • Refundable bidding deposit may apply
  • One account per user

4. Vehicle Eligibility

  • No vehicles older than 10 years unless exempt
  • Seller must confirm legal ownership or exemption

5. Auction Format

  • Timed online auctions
  • Auto-extend in final 2 minutes
  • Viewing available at designated locations

6. Reserve & Bidding

  • Reserve may be undisclosed
  • Highest bid at close wins subject to reserve

7. Payments & Fees

  • Payment within 24 hours
  • Buyer pays taxes & duties (ZIMRA)
  • Documentation fee may apply

8. Ownership Transfer

  • Transfer must be completed within 14 days
  • Seller provides registration book + CVR4 + ID docs
  • Buyer provides ID & proof of residence

9. Risk & Delivery

  • Risk passes on fall of hammer
  • Collection within 72 hours
  • Sold as-is condition

10. Consumer Rights

  • Cooling-off may apply under CPA
  • Auction exemption may require waiver
  • Return rules depend on legal classification

11. Default

  • Non-payment leads to forfeiture of deposit
  • Vehicle may be re-auctioned

12. Privacy

  • Data processed under Zimbabwe Data Protection Act
  • Marketing opt-out available

13. Governing Law

  • Laws of Zimbabwe
  • Jurisdiction: Harare courts
