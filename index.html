Auction Rules

BidUp Marketplace — Zimbabwe Vehicle Auctions v1.2

Disclaimer: This is a template for operational use. Final legal review by a Zimbabwean attorney is required. Must comply with Consumer Protection Act [Chapter 14:44], ZIMRA regulations, and CVR requirements.

1. Definitions

"Auctioneer" means BidUp Marketplace. "Buyer" means successful bidder. "Hammer Price" means final bid accepted. "Reserve" means confidential minimum price. "CVR4" means Change of Ownership form.

2. Legal Framework

All auctions governed by Zimbabwe law. Consumer Protection Act cooling-off rights apply to private buyers (7 days). Commercial buyers are excluded. ZIMRA VAT and presumptive tax obligations apply where relevant.

3. Registration to Bid

Bidders must provide National ID/Passport, proof of residence, and pay refundable $100 USD bidding deposit. BidUp verifies identity via KYC. Bidding constitutes legal offer to purchase.

4. Vehicle Eligibility & Disclosures

Vehicles must be: (a) less than 10 years old per SI 59/2026 unless exempt, (b) duty fully paid with proof, (c) ZRP police clearance valid, (d) roadworthy or sold "as is" with disclosure. Sellers warrant clear title, no encumbrances.

5. Auction Format

English ascending auction online. 5-minute anti-sniping: any bid in final 5 minutes extends auction by 5 minutes. Bids are in USD or ZiG at daily RBZ interbank rate +2%. Bids are binding.

6. Reserve Prices

Seller may set reserve. If not met, vehicle is "passed in". BidUp may negotiate post-auction within 48 hours.

7. Taxes, Duties & Fees

Buyer pays: Hammer price + 8% buyer's premium (capped $800) + $50 documentation fee + ZINARA transfer $30. Seller pays 5% commission (capped $500). All payments via BidUp escrow.

8. Change of Ownership

BidUp lodges CVR4 with Central Vehicle Registry. Seller must sign CVR4 and provide copy of ID. ZIMRA 14-day change of ownership notification is mandatory per seller. Buyer receives registration book within 14 working days after payment clearance.

9. Risk & Delivery

Risk passes to buyer on full payment. Vehicle must be collected within 72 hours from designated yard (Harare/Bulawayo). Storage $20/day after.

10. Cooling-Off Period

Per CPA Section 56, private consumers may cancel within 7 days if vehicle not collected and misrepresentation proven. Does NOT apply to commercial entities or auctions where reserve met and correctly described.

11. Default by Buyer or Seller

Buyer default: deposit forfeited, vehicle re-listed, buyer liable for deficiency. Seller default: $500 penalty, listing removed. BidUp reserves right to ban.

12. Privacy & Data

Personal data processed per Zimbabwe Data Protection Act. ID numbers encrypted. Vehicle history retained 7 years for ZIMRA audit.

13. Governing Law

Laws of Zimbabwe. Disputes: mediation first, then Harare Magistrates Court jurisdiction.
